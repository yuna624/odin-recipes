No-Bake Matcha Cheesecake

Description:

A slice of harmony

The crust crumbles like fallen leaves, giving way to velvet matcha cream‚Äîneither too sweet nor too bitter, but balanced as dawn light. Chilled to set its layers, this cake rewards those who wait.

Ingredients:

For the Crust:

  • 1 ¬Ω cups (150g) digestive biscuits (or graham crackers).
  • ¬º cup (55g) unsalted butter, melted.
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional).

For the Filling:

  • 2 cups (450g) cream cheese, softened.
  • ¬Ω cup (120ml) heavy cream, cold.
  • ‚Öî cup (80g) powdered sugar.
  • 2 tbsp high-quality matcha powder (sifted).
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract.
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (optional, for brightness).

For Topping(Optional):

  • Whipped cream.
  • Matcha powder for dusting.
  • Fresh berries.

Instructions:

  1. Make the Crust:
    1. Crush biscuits into fine crumbs (use a food processor or rolling pin).
    2. Mix with melted butter and sugar (if using). Press firmly into a 7-inch (18cm) springform pan or lined cake tin.
    3. Freeze for 15 mins to set.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    1. Whip the cream: Beat cold heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Set aside.
    2. Mix cream cheese: In another bowl, beat cream cheese, powdered sugar, matcha, and vanilla until smooth.
    3. Fold in whipped cream: Gently combine with the cream cheese mixture. Add lemon juice if using.
  3. Assemble & Chill:
    1. Pour filling over the crust. Smooth the top with a spatula.
    2. Chill for at least 6 hours (overnight is best!).
  4. Serve:
    • Dust with matcha powder, and add whipped cream or berries.
    • Slice with a hot knife for clean cuts.

  • Matcha quality matters! Use ceremonial grade for vibrant color and flavor.
  • No springform pan? Use a lined cake tin or individual jars.
  • Too bitter? Adjust sugar to taste (matcha can vary in intensity).

Variations:

  • Vegan: Use coconut cream + vegan cream cheese. Gluten-free: Swap biscuit
  • Gluten-free: Swap biscuits for almond flour crust (1 ¬Ω cups almond flour + 3 tbsp melted coconut oil).
  • Extra decadence: Add a white chocolate layer (melt 100g white chocolate, mix into filling).
